Why Consider Income Protection Cover?

According to figures published by The Health Foundation, around 2.5 million people in the United Kingdom live with a long term health condition that prevents them from working and earning a living—that’s approximately 1 in 15 working age adults. In addition, an estimated 1.2 million people experience a work limiting condition that directly affects the type or amount of work they can do.

This means that around 10% of the UK’s working-age population face income disruption due to health-related issues. When combined with the pressures of the cost of living crisis, it’s clear that having a tailored income protection policy in place can offer valuable financial support.At Wealthmax, we help individuals explore income protection options suited to their needs, providing guidance to support financial resilience during uncertain times.

Conditions Commonly Covered by Income Protection

Income protection provides a financial safety net if you're unable to work due to illness or injury for an extended period (typically four weeks or more). A standard income protection policy covers loss of income resulting from medical conditions but does not provide cover for unemployment due to redundancy or dismissal. If your GP confirms that you're unfit for work, you may be eligible to claim—subject to policy terms and any exclusions. However, securing the right level of cover that pays out when you need it is something best discussed with your Wealthmax adviser. Some of the most common reasons for income protection claims include:-

Accidents leading to disability

Torn ligaments, ruptured tendons and broken bones
